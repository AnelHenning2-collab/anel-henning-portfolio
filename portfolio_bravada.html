Aurum: Luxury Skincare Brand Identity System

Spec Work — Personal Project

The luxury skincare category is paradoxical to design for: the product's scientific credibility must be communicated visually by someone who has never touched a microscope. The result in most skincare branding is either too clinical (cold, pharmaceutical) or too indulgent (all texture, no substance). Aurum was my attempt to hold both.

The color system began with one decision: champagne over gold. Gold says wealth. Champagne says taste. The difference between them is about 30% less saturation and a shift in psychological register — from display to refinement. Everything else — the deep forest green for grounding, the matte black for authority, the ivory for space — was built around maintaining that distinction.

Deliverables include: primary logo system (AI/SVG/EPS/PNG, all sizes and color variations), packaging design (serum bottle dieline + 3D mockup in Adobe Dimension), brand guidelines PDF (12 pages, 8.5×11 in), Instagram feed aesthetic (Figma, 1080×1080), and editorial print ad (InDesign, 8.5×11 in, 300 dpi). Tools: Illustrator, Dimension, InDesign, Photoshop, Figma.

Logo Design Suite: Five Industries, One Standard

Spec Work — Personal Project

This project started as a personal answer to a question I kept asking myself: how do I know when a logo is actually finished? My answer was a test. A logo is finished when it works at 16 pixels — as a favicon, a social media profile picture, an app icon. If you can read the intent at 16px, you can read it anywhere. If you can't, the complexity is decorative, not communicative.

The five logos span industries deliberately chosen for their different visual conventions — wellness (soft, organic), fintech (precise, trustworthy), food and beverage (warm, approachable), fashion (minimal, authoritative), and professional services (stable, credible). The constraint was that each mark had to work in those conventions without becoming a cliché of them. Wellness should not be a leaf. Fintech should not be a circuit board.

Deliverables for each of the five logos: primary logo (AI/SVG/EPS), logomark/icon (AI/PNG/SVG), reversed versions (AI/PNG), and a usage spec sheet (InDesign/PDF). Mockup presentations at 1920×1080 px. Tools: Illustrator, InDesign, Photoshop.

Fuego Viejo: Tampa Artisan Hot Sauce Packaging Design

Spec Work — Personal Project

The hot sauce category is one of the most visually competitive in specialty food — every brand is screaming for attention with bright colors, skull imagery, and heat warnings designed to intimidate. Fuego Viejo takes the opposite position: the design is confident rather than loud, and the typography carries what a lesser brand would put in a flame icon.

The three-SKU system uses a consistent structural framework — same label format, same typeface hierarchy, same brand mark — with color and material texture shifting between tiers. The Original is warm and approachable (kraft cream, forest green). The Reserve is elevated and restrained (matte black, champagne). The Seasonal is expressive (the illustration changes with each release). One brand, three personalities, zero confusion about which is which.

Deliverables: label design for 3 SKUs (dieline template, 4×6 in, AI/PDF), 3D bottle mockups (Adobe Dimension), shelf tray POS display, e-commerce product images (2000×2000 px), and DTC brand story card (4×6 in, InDesign). Tools: Illustrator, Adobe Dimension, InDesign, Photoshop.

Artery Tampa — Brand Identity Refresh

The Brief

A Tampa arts nonprofit with 15 years of community history needed a brand refresh — not a rebrand. The existing mark had equity with longtime supporters and board members; throwing it away would alienate the base. The challenge: modernize the visual system enough to compete for younger donor attention and corporate sponsors without losing the soul of what the organization had built. In My Own Words

"A refresh is harder than a rebrand because you have to honor what already exists. I kept the original concept — the idea of creative connection — but rebuilt everything around it with tighter rules and better visual hierarchy. The before is not bad. The after is just more intentional."

Artery Tampa: Nonprofit Brand Identity Refresh

Spec Work — Personal Project

The most important thing I did at the start of this project was decide what not to change. Artery Tampa had 15 years of community trust embedded in their existing brand — their board members, longtime donors, and community partners all had a relationship with the mark. Throwing it away to start fresh would have been the easy creative choice, and the wrong one.

The refresh kept the conceptual foundation intact while rebuilding the execution from the ground up: tighter typography hierarchy, a more disciplined color system (forest green as anchor, champagne as accent, ivory as space), and a logo construction that scales cleanly from business card to event banner without losing legibility. The stationery suite, event poster, and donor report cover all apply the refreshed system with enough consistency to signal change and enough continuity to honor the history.

Deliverables: refreshed logo system (AI/SVG/EPS/PNG, all variants), stationery suite (letterhead 8.5×11 in, business card 3.5×2 in, InDesign/PDF), event poster (18×24 in, 150 dpi), social profile graphics (1080×1080, 820×312 px), and donor report cover (8.5×11 in, InDesign). Tools: Illustrator, InDesign, Photoshop.

Maison Noir: Fashion Pop-Up Brand Experience

Spec Work — Personal Project

A pop-up shop has approximately six weeks to establish itself as a destination — not just a temporary store. The brand experience has to do what years of brand building usually accomplish. Every touchpoint, from the first Instagram story to the shopping bag in someone's hand on the walk home, has to communicate the same thing: this is worth your attention.

Maison Noir's visual language is built on restraint as a signal of confidence. Matte black, ivory, and champagne. Classic serif for the brand name. Minimal copy on every touchpoint. The tissue paper pattern — a small repeated logomark at a 45-degree angle — is barely visible, but it is the thing customers feel when they unwrap a purchase. That moment is designed. The shopping bag is made to be carried visibly, photographed casually, and posted without any prompting. It is the most organic form of advertising available to a boutique, and it costs exactly as much as the bag itself.

Deliverables: storefront signage (48×72 in, 150 dpi, AI/PDF), shopping bag dieline, hang tag (2×3.5 in, 300 dpi, InDesign), tissue paper pattern (AI repeat), and Instagram Story template set (1080×1920 px, Figma). Tools: Illustrator, InDesign, Adobe Dimension, Figma.
