Photography Direction
✋
Hero Shot

Extreme close-up of skin texture — a cheekbone, a collarbone, the bridge of a nose. No full-face shots. Skin is the landscape, not the face.

☀️
Lighting

Natural window light, slightly overexposed. Let the highlights blow out softly. No studio strobes — this is a Sunday morning, not a photoshoot.

🎞
Color Grade

Warm and slightly desaturated. Pull the shadows into brown, not blue. Think film photograph left in the sun. Not Instagram-filtered.

❌
Never

Heavy retouching, pore-erasing, skin smoothing filters. The point is texture. A smoothed-out face defeats the entire campaign concept.

🏥
Subject Matter

Hands — a surgeon's gloved hands, a nurse's hands on a patient's arm. Hands communicate competence and care simultaneously. No happy stock photo hospital hallway shots.

💡
Lighting

Soft, directional natural light from a window. Not clinical overhead fluorescent. The light should say "this is a place where healing happens," not "this is an institution."

⚡
Hero Imagery

Light FX

Photography Direction — Editorial Luxury
🫙
Product Photography

Single bottle on matte black or ivory surface. One prop maximum — a sprig of botanical ingredient, a piece of raw material. Never multiple products in one frame.

🕯
Lighting

Chiaroscuro — strong directional light from one side, deep shadows on the other. The product should emerge from darkness, not sit in flat studio light. Think Vermeer, not Amazon product listing.

🌿
Texture + Material

Linen, marble, raw stone, botanical leaves — physical textures as background. Never printed paper, never plastic. The materials around the product signal the product's tier.

❌
Never

Bright studio white backgrounds, multiple products scattered, lifestyle photos of people using the product. The product does not need a person to validate it. It stands alone.

The Luxury Composition Rules — Apply to Every Bravada Piece
50% of every layout must be empty space — this is not waste, it is the design
Never center-align both the image AND the type — one must anchor left
One typeface maximum per layout — weight and size create hierarchy, not different fonts
CTA is always text + underline — never a filled button for luxury brands
Color palette: use maximum 3 colors per execution, 2 is better
Photography is full-bleed or not used — no boxed images with borders
Brand name always appears in champagne — never white, never black alone
Product name set in ultra-small ALL CAPS with wide tracking — it whispers, not shouts

Master Reference
Universal Ad Composition Rules
For Every Display Ad
→ Brand mark always visible, never smaller than 60px wide
→ One primary message per ad — never two headlines
→ One CTA button — never two calls to action
→ Minimum 16px padding on all sides for 300×250
For Every Print Piece
→ 0.125 in bleed on all sides — always
→ No critical content within 0.25 in of trim edge
→ Black text = 100K only, never CMYK rich black
→ Export PDF/X-1a for offset print, PDF/X-4 for digital print
For Social Media
→ Safe zone: keep all text within center 80% of frame
→ Text covers no more than 20% of image area (Meta rule)
→ Design at 2× resolution: 2160×2160 for 1080 output
→ Test at phone size: thumbnail must read in 2 seconds
