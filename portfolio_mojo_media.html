ClearShield Cybersecurity: B2B SaaS Landing Page

Spec Work — Personal Project

The brief for this landing page was deceptively simple: make a cybersecurity product feel approachable to non-technical small business owners without making it feel watered-down to technical IT buyers. These are two different people with two different fears — but they land on the same page.

The solution was hierarchy. The headline speaks to consequence (what happens if you don't act), the subheadline speaks to accessibility (no IT department required, 60 minutes), and the feature grid beneath speaks to specifics for the technical reader who scrolls. The CTA is "Request Your Free Demo" — not "Buy Now," not "Get a Quote." A demo is low-stakes, educational, and feels like the prospect's idea, not a sales push. The trust signals (SOC 2, no contract, free trial) address the three most common objections before the form is even visible.

Designed for HubSpot CMS (1440 px desktop, 375 px mobile-responsive), LinkedIn sponsored content (1200×627 px), and connected to a thank-you page and automated email nurture sequence. Tools: Figma, Adobe XD.

HR Tech Guide: Lead Magnet eBook Design

Spec Work — Personal Project

A lead magnet eBook is a trust transaction: the reader gives you their email address in exchange for expertise they believe is worth having. The design has to make that exchange feel worth it before the form is even filled out. The cover is a sales page in 8.5×11 inches.

I designed this guide to feel like something an HR leader would forward to their VP — not something they'd skim and delete. The cover uses deep navy and teal to signal authority and tech-forward positioning, while the interior typography prioritizes scanability. Most eBook readers spend 90 seconds with a download. Every spread was designed for the skimmer first, the reader second.

Deliverables: 12-page eBook (8.5×11 in, 150 dpi, InDesign), content offer landing page (Figma, 1440 px), LinkedIn sponsored post (1200×627 px), and social teaser card (1080×1080 px). Tools: InDesign, Figma, Illustrator.

Meridian Accounting: 5-Email Prospect Nurture Sequence

Spec Work — Personal Project

This nurture sequence solves a specific problem: a small business owner has shown interest (downloaded content, filled a form, attended a webinar), but they have not booked a consultation. They need to be reminded, reassured, and given a reason to act — five times, over two weeks, without feeling harassed.

Each email in the sequence has a single job. Email 1 acknowledges the pain. Email 2 provides social proof. Email 3 offers a specific, relevant insight. Email 4 handles the most common objection. Email 5 creates urgency. The design template serves all five: a consistent header (brand trust), a single-focus hero (the message), trust stats, and one CTA button. Nothing competes with the button.

Designed for HubSpot email (600 px, inline CSS, cross-client tested), with plain-text fallback and mobile-responsive layout (375 px breakpoint). Figma mockups for all 5 emails. Tools: Figma, HubSpot email editor.

Keystone Consulting: LinkedIn Content Template System

Spec Work — Personal Project

The hardest brief in template design is this: build something that a non-designer can operate daily, a brand manager can approve instantly, and a designer would not be embarrassed to put their name on. These three requirements pull in completely different directions. The solution is constraint — not as a limitation, but as the design itself.

The Keystone system includes 8 Figma templates covering every recurring content type: thought leadership statistics, case study snapshots, team spotlights, event announcements, service spotlights, and weekly insight cards. All templates share the same color system, type scale, and layout grid. Editable layers are labeled clearly. Everything else is locked. The coordinator changes three text fields and exports. The brand looks consistent every time.

Deliverables: 8-template Figma component library (1080×1080 px squares, 1200×627 px landscapes, 1080×1080 px PDF carousel format), plus a 1-page brand usage guide PDF. Tools: Figma, Illustrator.

Tampa Bay Commercial Real Estate: Q2 2026 Market Infographic

Spec Work — Personal Project

The best data visualization is invisible — the reader absorbs the insight without noticing the design. This infographic had to work for three different audiences with three different levels of expertise: the sophisticated investor reading specific vacancy rates, the business owner who just wants to know if it's a good time to sign a lease, and the developer skimming for construction pipeline data. The horizontal bar system serves all three because the relative length communicates directionally even without reading the numbers.

Every design choice was made in service of scanability at two scales: the full 8.5×11 inch print version and the 600×800 px email thumbnail. If a label or chart element didn't survive the thumbnail test, it was removed. The teal color system is deliberate — it signals trustworthy data (blue-adjacent) without the coldness of pure navy.

Designed in four formats: print (8.5×11 in, 300 dpi, InDesign), social (1080×1350 px, PNG), email insert (600×800 px), and presentation slide (1920×1080 px). Tools: Illustrator, InDesign.
