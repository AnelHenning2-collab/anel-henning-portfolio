Healthcare Campaign — Print + Digital
Tampa General Hospital — "When it matters most."

The Brief

Tampa General needed to reach families choosing a hospital for planned and emergency procedures — a high-stakes, emotional decision. The challenge: communicate excellence without sounding clinical. The campaign leads with experience metrics and human warmth simultaneously. Target: adults 35–60 making healthcare decisions for their family.

In My Own Words

"Healthcare design is one of the hardest categories — you are designing for someone's worst day. I wanted to build something that felt reassuring without feeling cold, human without being sentimental. The restraint in this layout is intentional — white space is the design equivalent of a calm voice."

Tampa General Hospital: When It Matters Most Campaign

Spec Work — Personal Project

Healthcare advertising asks designers to solve an impossible tension: communicate clinical excellence to someone who is frightened. The instinct is to soften everything — warm colors, smiling families, soft focus. I took the opposite approach. This campaign trusts the audience to respond to clarity and confidence.

The headline does the work a stock photo usually fails to do: it quantifies experience in human terms ("ten thousand times") rather than abstract rankings. The layout gives that headline room to breathe — white space is not emptiness here, it is the visual equivalent of a steady hand.

Campaign designed for print (magazine full page at 8.5×11 in / 300 dpi, newspaper half page at 10.5×7 in), Google Display Network (728×90, 300×250), and broadcast television storyboard (1920×1080 frames). Tools: InDesign, Photoshop, Illustrator.

Altura Waterfront Residences: Luxury Real Estate Campaign

Spec Work — Personal Project

The instinct in luxury real estate advertising is to fill every inch with photography — gleaming kitchens, infinity pools, sunset terraces. This campaign deliberately resisted that. The insight is that true luxury buyers are tired of being sold to. They respond to restraint, to space, to the confidence of a brand that doesn't need to oversell.

The tagline — "This is not a home. This is a vantage point." — reframes the purchase entirely. You are not buying square footage. You are buying perspective. The deep navy and gold color system signals premium without being ostentatious, and the typography is given as much room as an architectural rendering.

Campaign assets: magazine full page (8.5×11 in, 300 dpi), Google Display half page (300×600 px), billboard (14×48 ft ratio, PDF), direct mail postcard (5.5×8.5 in), and programmatic billboard banner (970×250 px). Tools: InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop.

Behance Project Description — Copy & Paste Ready

Tampa Bay Lightning: Season Opener Campaign

Spec Work — Personal Project

Hockey arena advertising is a design sprint. You have a driver doing 70 mph, a fan walking past a bus shelter, a viewer glancing up at an arena board. Every format gives you under two seconds. The entire creative challenge is to make those two seconds feel electric — literally.

The design language takes the Lightning's bolt and turns it from a logo element into a light source. Motion blur, high contrast between the deep cobalt and ice blue, and a headline that reads in a single breath: "The ice is back. Are you?" It is a question, not a statement — it creates the psychological need to answer yes.

Campaign assets: highway billboard (14×48 ft ratio, AI/PDF), bus shelter (68.5×47.5 in, 150 dpi), digital arena display (1920×1080 px, MP4), Facebook event ad (1200×628 px), and leaderboard banner (728×90 px). Tools: Photoshop, Illustrator, After Effects.

Tampa Bay Literacy Coalition: 2025 Annual Impact Report

Spec Work — Personal Project

The annual report is the nonprofit sector's most important design artifact — it speaks to donors who already believe in the mission and to grant committees who are evaluating it for the first time. The design has to serve both audiences without compromising either.

My approach was to make the statistics feel specific rather than impressive. Instead of leading with total numbers, each data point is anchored to a human consequence: 14,820 adults served is not a program metric — it is 14,820 people who can now read their child's school notice, their prescription label, their ballot. The data visualization uses a restrained palette and generous white space to give the numbers room to land.

Designed as a 20-page print report (8.5×11 in cover, 17×11 in spreads, 300 dpi), digital interactive PDF, LinkedIn social impact cards (1080×1080), and email announcement header (600×300 px). Tools: InDesign, Illustrator, Photoshop.

Palma Coffee Co.: Local Brand Rebrand + Campaign

Spec Work — Personal Project

The rebrand brief for Palma Coffee Co. had one non-negotiable: the new identity had to work at three scales simultaneously — a coffee cup in someone's hand, a storefront sign above a Seminole Heights doorway, and a 1080×1080 Instagram post on a phone screen. Most small business rebrands fail because they design for one of these and hope the others work themselves out. This system was built for all three from the first sketch.

The color palette is derived from the roast — dark espresso brown, warm terracotta, cream — grounded in the actual product rather than trend. The typography pairs a classic serif (for the logo) with clean sans-serif (for body and digital use), giving the brand the authority of something established without feeling dated.

Deliverables: logo system (vector AI/SVG), cup sleeve dieline, storefront signage (48×72 in, 150 dpi), Instagram feed grid (1080×1080 px, 6-panel concept), and newspaper strip ad (5×2 in, 300 dpi). Tools: Illustrator, Photoshop, InDesign.
